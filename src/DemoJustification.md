> > The two test cases I chose were based off of my personal difficulties that I've faced when doing photography. When trying to freeze an object in motion, a very quick shutter speed is needed, and compensating with ISO and aperture can be difficult, especially when it's already sunny. Additionally, shooting in low-light has always been difficult for me, and it's especially harder when not shooting on a full-frame camera. Balancing use of ISO without too much noise along with SS and aperture can cause a lot of problems. When shooting handheld, you have to be mindful of how long you make your shutter speed before it begins to blur from your hands twitching.
